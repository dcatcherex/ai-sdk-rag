# Escalation and Emergency Guidance

Use this file when a farmer needs help beyond chat triage.

## Contact local agricultural support when

- disease or insect damage spreads quickly;
- symptoms appear in many plots or villages;
- roots, stems, trunks, or fruit are rotting;
- cassava mosaic, sugarcane white leaf, or other viral/phytoplasma disease is suspected;
- a high-value crop such as durian, mango, or vegetables near harvest is affected;
- pesticide choice, label legality, harvest interval, or residue risk is unclear;
- the farmer plans to sell produce soon after spraying;
- a banned or suspicious pesticide product is involved.

## Chemical exposure emergency

If a person may have been exposed to pesticide:

1. Stop pesticide handling immediately.
2. Move away from the treated area.
3. Remove contaminated clothing if safe.
4. Rinse skin or eyes with clean water.
5. Call 1669 or go to hospital immediately.
6. Bring the pesticide container or label if safe.
