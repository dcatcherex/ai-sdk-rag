# Field Diagnosis Checklist for Thai Crop Pest and Disease Triage

Use this checklist before giving advice to Thai farmers. It is designed for cautious field triage, not laboratory diagnosis.

## First-pass triage

1. Identify the crop.
2. Identify the affected part: leaf, shoot, stem, root, flower, fruit, panicle, tuber, or whole plant.
3. Describe the symptom pattern:
   - spots, rings, blight, scorch, mosaic, wilting, rot, powder, mold, galls, bore holes, frass, webbing, insects, mites, or honeydew.
4. Check spread pattern:
   - one plant, one row, patchy area, low wet area, field edge, whole field, or after spraying/fertilizing.
5. Check recent conditions:
   - heavy rain, standing water, drought, high humidity, cold nights, recent pesticide/fertilizer use, new seedlings/cuttings, nearby infected fields.
6. Separate likely causes:
   - fungal/oomycete disease: wet conditions, leaf spots/blight/rot, visible mold or rapid decay.
   - bacterial disease: water-soaked lesions, ooze, bad smell, fast spread in wet weather.
   - virus/phytoplasma: mosaic, yellowing, leaf curl, stunting, abnormal shoots; often no curative treatment.
   - insect/mite: visible insects, eggs, webbing, frass, bore holes, rasping, honeydew, sooty mold.
   - nutrient/water/chemical stress: uniform pattern, tied to soil condition, spray event, drought, waterlogging, or field zone.

## Confidence levels

Use these labels:

- High: crop is known, symptoms are distinctive, and key risk conditions match.
- Medium: likely pattern matches, but 1-2 similar causes remain possible.
- Low: crop or symptom details are missing, photo is unclear, or damage could be non-biological.

Never state a definitive diagnosis from one photo or a short message.

## Minimum farmer-safe answer

Even when uncertain, give safe next steps:

- Mark affected area and monitor spread for 24-48 hours.
- Remove or destroy badly affected plant material only when disease spread risk is high.
- Improve drainage and airflow when rot, mold, or wet-weather disease is suspected.
- Inspect the underside of leaves, shoots, fruits, and soil line for insects, mites, eggs, frass, or mold.
- Avoid spraying multiple chemicals before the problem is clearer.
- For virus/phytoplasma suspicion, do not move cuttings, seedlings, or plant parts from the affected plot.

## What to ask if one question is needed

Ask only one critical question. Prefer:

- "พืชอะไร และอาการเริ่มที่ใบ ลำต้น ราก หรือผล?"
- "อาการลามเร็วไหม หรือเป็นเฉพาะบางต้น?"
- "มีแมลง ไข่ หนอน มูลหนอน หรือใยใต้ใบไหม?"
- "ก่อนเกิดอาการมีฝนหนัก น้ำขัง หรือเพิ่งพ่นสารไหม?"

## Escalation triggers

Tell the farmer to contact the local agricultural extension officer when:

- symptoms spread fast across the field;
- whole field or high-value crop is affected;
- root/stem rot is severe;
- viral disease is suspected in cassava, sugarcane, vegetables, or nursery plants;
- diagnosis is unclear after checking symptoms;
- pesticide use is being considered for food crops near harvest;
- the farmer has symptoms of chemical exposure.
