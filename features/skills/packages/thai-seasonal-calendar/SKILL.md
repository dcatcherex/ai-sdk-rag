# Thai Seasonal Marketing Calendar

Use this calendar to anchor content in real Thai cultural moments. Good seasonal content feels timely and locally relevant — not like a generic template with a date slapped on.

When you pull a date from this skill:
1. Check which holidays/events fall within the user's target window (next 2–4 weeks is the sweet spot)
2. Pick the 1–2 most relevant events for their brand's products, tone, and audience
3. Build content around the emotional or cultural core of the event — not just "happy [holiday]!"
4. If the user's brand has a `platforms` field in brand profile, tailor the format accordingly

## Using the calendar

For date calculations involving the Thai lunar calendar, estimate from the reference dates below — lunar dates shift by ±2 weeks year to year. When precision matters, tell the user to verify the exact lunar date for the current year.

## Quick-pick by brand type

**Fashion / beauty / skincare**
High-impact windows: Valentine's, Songkran (summer skin care), Mother's Day, 11.11, Christmas/NYE

**Food & beverage**
High-impact windows: Songkran, Loy Krathong, Vegetarian Festival, all major holidays (gifting season), 12.12

**Agriculture / fresh produce**
High-impact windows: harvest seasons by crop, Songkran (water = abundance), King's Birthday (Dec 5 — often tied to royal agricultural projects)

**Education / courses / coaching**
High-impact windows: school year start (May, Nov), New Year (resolution season), Children's Day (Jan)

**Health / wellness**
High-impact windows: New Year (Jan), Buddhist Lent (alcohol-free period = health awareness), World Health Day (Apr 7), Vegetarian Festival (Oct)

**Home / lifestyle**
High-impact windows: Songkran (spring cleaning angle), New Year (refresh/redecorate), 11.11 / 12.12

## Content angles that work across Thai holidays

- **Giving / merit-making** — Thais give gifts at nearly every major holiday. Lead with "give" messaging.
- **Family togetherness** — Songkran, New Year, Mother's/Father's Day are all about family. Emotional storytelling works.
- **Limited time / scarcity** — Thai consumers respond to "เฉพาะช่วงนี้เท่านั้น" (this period only).
- **Blessing language** — "ขอให้โชคดี", "อวยพร", "มีความสุข" — warm, sincere, not overly commercial.
- **Royal occasion respect** — On days connected to the monarchy (Coronation, King's/Queen's Birthday), keep tone warm and respectful. Avoid aggressive sale messaging.
